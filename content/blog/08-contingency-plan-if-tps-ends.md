# If Haiti TPS Ends: A Contingency Plan for Haitian TPS Holders

**By HCC Content Intelligence Pipeline · July 13, 2026**
**Status: DRAFT — FOR REVIEW**

---

The Miot v. Trump court stay is keeping Haiti TPS alive. But E-Verify calls it "limited relief" — and the Supreme Court's Mullin ruling could change the calculus. If the stay lifts, Haiti TPS ends immediately.

Here is exactly what you should do **now**, while you still have status, to prepare.

## Step 1: Document Everything

Before anything changes, get your paperwork in order:

- **Print your current EAD** (front and back)
- **Print your most recent I-797 approval notice** (Form I-821 approval)
- **Print the USCIS Haiti TPS page** showing current status
- **Print the E-Verify Haiti TPS Alert** (July 10, 2026)
- **Get certified copies** of your passport, birth certificate, and any Haitian government documents

Keep a physical folder. Digital copies too. You may need to prove your status history later.

## Step 2: Check Your Immigration Timeline

The USCIS Haiti TPS page notes:

> "Being granted and maintaining TPS status until a reasonable period before the filing of the asylum application is considered an extraordinary circumstance for the purposes of the one year filing deadline."

Translation: **TPS stops the asylum clock.** If you've been on TPS, you may still be eligible to apply for asylum even if you've been in the U.S. for years.

But this gets complicated fast. You need:

- The exact date you entered the United States
- Your TPS grant dates
- Any other immigration applications you've filed

**Talk to an accredited representative or immigration attorney now** — don't wait until TPS ends.

## Step 3: Explore Every Immigration Pathway

TPS is not the only status. Here are other options to investigate:

| Pathway | Who It's For | Timeline |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Asylum** | Fear of persecution in Haiti | File within "reasonable period" after TPS |
| **Family petition** | U.S. citizen or LPR relative | Years-long wait, but start now |
| **Employment-based** | Job offer from U.S. employer | Requires employer sponsorship |
| **U visa** | Victim of certain crimes | Must have cooperated with law enforcement |
| **VAWA** | Victim of domestic violence by U.S. citizen/LPR spouse | Self-petition, no sponsor needed |
| **HR9494 (if passed)** | Haitian nationals meeting criteria | Would create direct green card path |

## Step 4: Find Accredited Legal Help

Do not pay a notario. Do not pay someone who promises to "fix" your papers. Only use:

- **DOJ-accredited representatives** working for recognized organizations
- **Licensed immigration attorneys** (check your state bar)

Find free or low-cost help:
- **USCIS legal services finder:** [uscis.gov/avoid-scams/find-legal-services](https://www.uscis.gov/scams-fraud-and-misconduct/avoid-scams/find-legal-services)
- **DOJ recognized organizations:** [justice.gov/eoir/recognized-organizations-and-accredited-representatives](https://www.justice.gov/eoir/recognized-organizations-and-accredited-representatives-roster-state)

## Step 5: Build a Financial Buffer

If TPS ends and you lose work authorization:

- Save what you can now
- Know your state's rules on driver's licenses without federal status
- Identify community resources — churches, diaspora organizations, mutual aid networks
- If you have children who are U.S. citizens, understand their rights are unaffected

## Step 6: Advocate

Individual preparation matters. But collective action changes policy:

- **Call your Senators** about HR1689 (mandatory Haiti TPS) and HR9494 (permanent residence)
- **Submit a comment** on the discretionary EAD rule (docket USCIS-2026-0067, due Aug 4)
- **Join diaspora advocacy** — numbers create political pressure

## The Bottom Line

Haiti TPS could end quickly. Preparing now — while you have documentation, income, and status — is vastly easier than scrambling after a termination notice. Every step you take today is insurance against tomorrow.
