# A Republican Co-Sponsored This Haiti TPS Bill. Here's Why HR1689 Matters.

**By HCC Content Intelligence Pipeline · July 13, 2026**

---

In a Congress where immigration bills rarely cross party lines, HR1689 is different. It has a Republican cosponsor.

The **Haiti TPS Designation Requirement Act**, introduced by Rep. Laura Gillen (D-NY-4), would do exactly what its name says: **require** the Secretary of Homeland Security to designate Haiti for Temporary Protected Status. Not recommend. Not suggest. **Require.**

## The Bipartisan Signal

Rep. **Mike Lawler** (R-NY-17) signed on as a cosponsor on day one — February 27, 2025. In a House where Republican votes on immigration are rare, this is significant.

## Where It Stands

HR1689 has **already passed the House**. It was read the second time in the Senate on April 21, 2026 and placed on the **Senate Legislative Calendar under General Orders (Calendar No. 374).**

Translation: it's in the queue. The Senate can take it up at any time.

## Why This Bill Exists

DHS Secretary Kristi Noem terminated Haiti's TPS designation on November 28, 2025. The termination is currently blocked by a court order in *Miot v. Trump*, but that stay is described as "limited relief" by E-Verify. If the court lifts the stay, Haiti TPS ends immediately.

HR1689 would take that decision out of DHS's hands. If passed, the Secretary **must** designate Haiti for TPS — full stop.
