# Haiti TPS Dates Are Confusing. Here's What Each One Actually Means.

**By HCC Content Intelligence Pipeline · July 13, 2026**
**Status: DRAFT — FOR REVIEW**

---

If you've seen different expiration dates for Haiti TPS — February 3, July 24, August 3 — and wondered which one is real, you're not alone. The timeline is genuinely confusing because it changed multiple times. Here's the definitive explanation.

## The Three Dates, Explained

### August 3, 2025 — The Original Expiration

This was the expiration date set by the Biden administration's July 2024 extension and redesignation of Haiti TPS (89 FR 54484). It was the "normal" expiration.

### February 20, 2025 — The Partial Vacatur

On February 20, 2025, Secretary Noem **partially vacated** the July 2024 notice. This shortened the TPS designation and registration periods. It was an early signal that the new administration was taking a different approach to Haiti TPS.

### February 3, 2026 — The Termination Date

After reviewing country conditions, Secretary Noem determined Haiti no longer met TPS criteria. The Federal Register published the termination on November 28, 2025 (90 FR 54733) with an effective date of **February 3, 2026 at 11:59 PM.** This superseded a previous termination notice from July 1, 2025 (90 FR 28760).

**This is the date that the court blocked.**

### July 24, 2026 — The I-9 Date (Not the TPS Date)

This is where the confusion peaks. **July 24, 2026 is NOT a TPS expiration date.** It is a Form I-9 and E-Verify date.

Per the July 10, 2026 E-Verify alert, employers should enter "July 24, 2026" in Section 2 of Form I-9 and in E-Verify cases. This is a **procedural date for paperwork purposes** — it does not mean your TPS expires on July 24.

The E-Verify alert also says to write "as per court order" in Section 1, which is the more accurate reflection of the legal reality.

## The TL;DR

| Date | What It Means | Status |
|------|---------------|--------|
| Aug 3, 2025 | Biden-era expiration | Superseded by subsequent actions |
| Feb 20, 2025 | Noem partial vacatur | Took effect, shortened designation |
| Feb 3, 2026 | DHS termination date | **Blocked by court** (Miot v. Trump) |
| Jul 24, 2026 | I-9/E-Verify procedural date | For employer paperwork only |

## The Real Answer: "As Per Court Order"

The most accurate answer to "when does my TPS expire?" is: **it depends on the court.**

The *Miot v. Trump* stay is in effect. The Supreme Court's *Mullin* ruling may change things. Until the D.C. district court issues a new ruling, your TPS and EAD remain valid.

That's why E-Verify itself says to write **"as per court order"** — because no fixed date captures the legal reality right now.

## What to Do

1. **Don't panic about July 24** — it's a paperwork date, not an expiration
2. **Use "as per court order"** for Section 1 of Form I-9
3. **Monitor the USCIS Haiti TPS page** — any real change will appear there first
4. **Don't rely on dates you hear from friends or social media** — only .gov sources
