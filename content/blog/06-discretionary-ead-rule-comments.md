# TPS Holders: A Proposed Rule Could Restrict Work Permits — Comments Due August 4

**By HCC Content Intelligence Pipeline · July 13, 2026**
**Status: DRAFT — FOR REVIEW**

---

The Department of Homeland Security has proposed a rule that could make it harder for certain immigrants — including some TPS holders — to get or renew work permits. The public comment period closes **August 4, 2026**. This is an actionable deadline.

## What the Proposed Rule Does

Published in the Federal Register on June 5, 2026 (FR Doc 2026-11285), the rule would:

- **Limit eligibility** for "discretionary" employment authorization — the category that covers people granted humanitarian parole, deferred action, or released on an order of supervision
- **Clarify** when DHS will exercise its discretion to grant work permits
- **Specify** that people "who admit to committing acts that pose a threat to public safety or national security" may be denied

## How This Could Affect Haitian TPS Holders

Most Haitian TPS holders get their work permits through the **TPS-based EAD** category (category A12 or C19 on the EAD), not through "discretionary" authorization. So the direct impact may be limited.

But here's the concern: **if Haiti TPS is terminated** and the court stay lifts, many Haitian TPS holders would need to seek other forms of work authorization — exactly the "discretionary" categories this rule would restrict.

In other words: this rule may not affect you *now*, but it could affect you *later* — and later could come fast.

## Why Comments Matter

Federal agencies are required to read and respond to substantive public comments before finalizing a rule. Well-reasoned comments can:

- Change specific provisions
- Add exceptions for vulnerable populations
- Delay or accelerate the rule's timeline
- Create a record for future legal challenges

## How to Submit a Comment

1. Go to **[regulations.gov](https://www.regulations.gov)**
2. Search for **Docket ID: USCIS-2026-0067** (or search "Discretionary Employment Authorization")
3. Click "Comment"
4. Write your comment — even a paragraph matters
5. Submit before **August 4, 2026**

## What to Say

Your comment doesn't need to be legal analysis. Tell your story. Effective comments include:

- **Who you are** — "I am a Haitian TPS holder working as a [job] in [city]"
- **What's at stake** — "If I lose my work permit, my family loses our income"
- **What you want** — "Please ensure TPS holders are not caught by this rule if TPS is terminated"
- **Be specific** — Reference the docket number (USCIS-2026-0067)

## The Bigger Picture

This rule is part of a broader DHS effort to tighten work authorization pathways. Combined with the TPS terminations, it signals that **relying on discretionary work permits is becoming riskier.** The safest path is legislation — HR9494 would give Haitian nationals a direct route to permanent residence without needing discretionary EADs.
