# HR9494: A Real Path to a Green Card for Haitians — What the Haitian Refugee Immigration Fairness Act Would Do

**By HCC Content Intelligence Pipeline · July 13, 2026**

---

On June 25, 2026, Rep. Frederica Wilson (D-FL) introduced a bill that could change everything for Haitian nationals living in the United States.

The **Haitian Refugee Immigration Fairness Act of 2026 (HR9494)** would create a direct path to lawful permanent residence — a green card — for certain Haitian nationals. No lottery. No sponsorship requirement. Just an application.

## What the Bill Would Do

HR9494 would require the Secretary of Homeland Security to adjust qualifying Haitian nationals to permanent resident status if they:

1. **Apply within 3 years** of the bill becoming law
2. **Are admissible** to the United States for permanent residence

Critically, the bill **waives** several grounds of inadmissibility that normally block people from getting green cards:

- **Public charge** (Section 212(a)(4)) — you won't be denied for being low-income
- **Labor certification** (Section 212(a)(5)) — no job offer or PERM required
- **Illegal entry** (Section 212(a)(6)(A)) — past entry without inspection is waived
- **Documentation requirements** (Section 212(a)(7)(A)) — missing documents won't block you
- **Foreign residence** (Section 212(a)(9)) — prior removal orders may be waived

## Who Would NOT Qualify

The bill excludes people who:
- Have been convicted of an **aggravated felony**
- Have been convicted of **two or more crimes involving moral turpitude** (excluding purely political offenses)
- Have **ordered, incited, assisted, or participated in persecution** of any person

## Who's Behind It

Rep. Frederica Wilson introduced the bill with 11 cosponsors, including Rep. Sheila Cherfilus-McCormick (D-FL), the only Haitian-American member of Congress.

## Where It Stands

The bill has been **referred to the House Committee on the Judiciary** — the first step in the legislative process. It needs to pass committee, then the full House, then the Senate, then be signed by the President.

**This is early.** But it's the most ambitious Haiti immigration bill introduced this Congress.
