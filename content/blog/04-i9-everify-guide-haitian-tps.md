# Haitian TPS Holders: The I-9 & E-Verify Guide Your Employer Needs to See

**By HCC Content Intelligence Pipeline · July 13, 2026**

---

On July 10, 2026, E-Verify released a long-awaited operational alert specifically for Haitian TPS holders. If your employer has been confused about your work authorization, this guide is for them — and for you.

## The Problem

Many employers see an expired date on an Employment Authorization Document (EAD) and panic. They don't know that Haitian TPS EADs have been **automatically extended by court order.** The result? People losing jobs over paperwork confusion.

## The Official Guidance

Here is exactly what E-Verify says, copied from the July 10, 2026 alert:

### For Paper Form I-9

**Section 1 — Employee section:**
- In the "Expiration Date (if any)" field, write: **as per court order**

**Section 2 — Employer section:**
- In the "Expiration Date (if any)" field, write: **July 24, 2026**
- In the "Additional Information" box, add a note explaining the court order extension

### For E-Verify Cases

When creating an E-Verify case for a Haitian TPS holder:
- Enter the expiration date from Section 2 of the Form I-9: **July 24, 2026**

### Documentation

E-Verify instructs employers to:
> "Download the Alert and TPS Haiti webpage and attach them to Form I-9."

This means the employer should print both documents and staple them to the I-9.

## Which EADs Are Extended?

If your EAD has **any** of these original expiration dates, it is automatically extended:

> Feb 3, 2026 · Aug 3, 2025 · Aug 3, 2024 · Jun 30, 2024  
> Feb 3, 2023 · Dec 31, 2022 · Oct 4, 2021 · Jan 4, 2021  
> Jan 2, 2020 · Jul 22, 2019 · Jan 22, 2018 · Jul 22, 2017

## The Fine Print

The E-Verify alert includes this important caveat:

> "The extension is limited relief until the lower courts align with the U.S. Supreme Court's favorable decision in *Mullin v. ____* (2026), issued on June 25, 2026."

This means the extension depends on a court order that could change. E-Verify also warns: **"Check back to USCIS websites regularly for updated information."**

## What to Bring to Your Employer

Print these two pages and bring them together:

1. **E-Verify Haiti TPS Alert:** [e-verify.gov/about-e-verify/whats-new/update-on-termination-of-temporary-protected-status-for-haiti-release-0](https://www.e-verify.gov/about-e-verify/whats-new/update-on-termination-of-temporary-protected-status-for-haiti-release-0)

2. **USCIS Haiti TPS Page:** [uscis.gov/humanitarian/temporary-protected-status/temporary-protected-status-designated-country-haiti](https://www.uscis.gov/humanitarian/temporary-protected-status/temporary-protected-status-designated-country-haiti)

## If Your Employer Refuses

Some employers may still refuse to accept the "as per court order" notation. If this happens:

- Contact the **Department of Justice Immigrant and Employee Rights Section** at [justice.gov/ier](https://www.justice.gov/ier)
- Document everything — write down who you spoke to, when, and what they said
- File a charge of discrimination if your employment is terminated over valid work authorization
