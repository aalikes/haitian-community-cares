A federal court order has extended work permits for approximately 350,000 Haitian Temporary Protected Status (TPS) holders through July 24, 2026, providing a critical last-minute reprieve for families facing deportation uncertainty.

The extension comes as a federal judge continues to weigh the legality of the Department of Homeland Security's termination of Haiti's TPS designation. The case, *Miot et al. v. Trump et al.*, has become the focal point of a nationwide advocacy campaign led by Haitian community organizations, immigration lawyers, and members of Congress.

"This extension gives our community breathing room, but it is not a permanent solution," said advocates who have been rallying in Miami, New York, Springfield, and other cities with large Haitian populations. "We need Congress to act."

USCIS confirmed that Employment Authorization Documents (EADs) with expiration dates linked to the Haiti TPS program remain automatically extended per the court order. Employers are urged to accept these extended documents as valid proof of work authorization.

Communities in South Florida — home to one of the largest Haitian diaspora populations in the United States — have organized emergency legal clinics to help TPS holders understand their rights and prepare documentation in case protections are not renewed beyond July 24.

**What to do now:**
- Check your EAD expiration date and confirm it's covered by the automatic extension
- Attend a legal clinic in your area
- Contact your congressional representatives to demand a permanent legislative solution
