## Current Status: Protected, But Precarious

Haitian TPS **remains in effect** thanks to the Miot court order. Your Employment Authorization Documents are automatically extended if they carry any of these expiration dates:

> Feb 3, 2026 · Aug 3, 2025 · Aug 3, 2024 · Jun 30, 2024 · Feb 3, 2023 · Dec 31, 2022 · Oct 4, 2021 · Jan 4, 2021 · Jan 2, 2020 · Jul 22, 2019 · Jan 22, 2018 · Jul 22, 2017

But E-Verify describes this as **"limited relief until the lower courts align with the Supreme Court's decision"** — meaning protection could end rapidly once the D.C. district court reviews SCOTUS.

## Form I-9: What to Tell Your Employer

Per the E-Verify Haiti TPS Alert (July 10, 2026):

- **Section 1:** Enter **"as per court order"** as the expiration date
- **Section 2:** Enter **"July 24, 2026"** and add a note in the Additional Information box
- **E-Verify cases:** Use **"July 24, 2026"** as the expiration date

**Print the official alert** from [e-verify.gov](https://www.e-verify.gov/about-e-verify/whats-new/update-on-termination-of-temporary-protected-status-for-haiti-release-0) and bring it to your employer.

## Congress Is Moving

**HR1689 — Haiti TPS Designation Requirement Act**  
Introduced by Rep. Laura Gillen (D-NY) with **bipartisan support** including Rep. Mike Lawler (R-NY). Would *require* DHS to designate Haiti for TPS. Passed the House. Now on Senate Calendar No. 374.

**HR9494 — Haitian Refugee Immigration Fairness Act of 2026**  
Introduced June 25, 2026 by Rep. Frederica Wilson (D-FL) with 12 cosponsors. Creates a **path to permanent residence** for certain Haitian nationals. Waives several inadmissibility grounds. 3-year application window. In House Judiciary Committee.
